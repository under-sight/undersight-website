The Problem

Good deals are getting killed by rigid rules

In private credit, speed and accuracy determine who wins. Yet most underwriting still relies on deterministic rule sets and manual review processes that were designed for a different era.

When a small business owner applied for funding through Chat Advance's marketplace, the application was auto-declined within seconds. A rules-based underwriting engine evaluated the deal against a fixed set of criteria and determined it didn't qualify.

But the numbers told a different story. Revenue was consistent. Bank data showed healthy cash flow patterns. The business had been operating for years. The decline wasn't a reflection of actual risk — it was a reflection of the limitations of a system that can only say yes or no, with no room for nuance.

"The rules engine couldn't see what was actually there. It checked boxes, not patterns. When the deal didn't fit the template, it was rejected automatically — regardless of the underlying fundamentals."

Sajit Roshan, from Deterministic Scorecards to Agentic Credit Assessments

The Solution

Agentic underwriting with undersight

Chat Advance integrated the undersight platform into their intake workflow. Instead of relying solely on rules-based filters, every application now passes through an AI-powered pipeline that combines document intelligence, enrichment, and risk assessment. Here's what happened when the previously declined applicant re-engaged.

Applicant engages via chat with Macy, the AI intake agent

The applicant started a conversation through Chat Advance's web channel. Macy, the AI intake agent, guided the conversation naturally — collecting application details, requesting bank statements, and clarifying information gaps in real time. No forms, no uploads, no waiting.

2

Bank data ingested via Plaid and enriched automatically

Bank statements were connected via Plaid and processed instantly. The undersight platform parsed transaction-level data into structured intelligence: revenue patterns, existing debt positions, seasonal trends, and anomaly signals. What would take an analyst 20+ minutes was completed in seconds.

3

RFIs addressed directly with the applicant by the agent

When additional information was needed, Macy asked the applicant directly. Missing data points, clarifications on specific transactions, and supplementary documentation were all resolved within the same conversation — no email chains, no delays.

4

Matching funders receive a rich submission package

The completed application was assembled into a comprehensive submission package: structured financial data, enriched risk signals, and supporting documentation. The package was matched to two funders whose criteria aligned with the deal profile. Both received the submission simultaneously.
