Customer stories·May 12, 2026·8 min read

How Chat Advance cut their deal cycle by 71% in twelve weeks.

What happens when document review stops being a bottleneck. A close look at the underwriting workflow before and after, with the metrics our team tracked weekly.

71%

Chat Advance came to us with a problem we'd seen a hundred times: deals were stalling not because they were bad, but because nobody had time to look at them. A four-person underwriting team was reading PDFs, copying numbers into spreadsheets, and chasing brokers for missing pages. Same-day decisions were a goal, never a default.

The before picture

Every application went through three manual passes — intake screening, transaction analysis, and committee review — with a median turnaround of three days. The team's bottleneck wasn't decision quality. It was data quality.

"We were spending 70% of our underwriting time on document handling and 30% on actual judgment. We knew the ratio had to flip."

What changed

We deployed underchat as the front door — handling intake, document collection, and RFI follow-ups across web and SMS — and pushed every clean submission through underscore for an initial composite grade and MCA detection.

  • Intake automation: Macy replaced the email back-and-forth. Median time from first message to complete package dropped from 41 hours to 9.
  • Scoring at the door: underscore returned a graded scorecard in under three seconds, eliminating the "first read" pass entirely.
  • Co-pilot for committee: Underwriters used chat-based deal Q&A to interrogate the package — answers came back with inline citations.

The results

Twelve weeks in, the headline numbers were already striking: 71% reduction in deal cycle time, 22% more booked receivables at constant approval volume, and zero added headcount. But the metric the team cares about most isn't on the deck — it's how many evenings their underwriters spend at their desks.

What we'd do differently

The fastest unlock turned out to be the brokerage-facing intake flow, not the internal copilot. If we were starting over, we'd ship that first and let everything else follow from clean data.
